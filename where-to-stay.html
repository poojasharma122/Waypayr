Where to Stay in New Jersey

From Casino Resorts to Quiet Inns — Stays That Actually Suit Your Trip

No matter where you're heading in New Jersey, the place you stay can shape the entire trip. This guide breaks things down by destination — with hotel recommendations that match the feel of the town and the kind of trip you're planning. Every option here is worth booking, and you won’t find filler listings just for the sake of variety.

Atlantic City

Borgata Hotel Casino & Spa

Atlantic City’s premier resort for luxury, dining, and nightlife. Located in the Marina District, Borgata offers expansive rooms, top-tier restaurants, a large gaming floor, and one of the best spas in the region.

Ocean Casino Resort

A sleek, glass-fronted resort on the north end of the boardwalk. Massive windows bring in ocean views from every corner, and the rooms are clean, modern, and made for longer stays.

Hard Rock Hotel & Casino

Centrally located on the boardwalk, Hard Rock is ideal for travellers who want music, energy, and entertainment on demand. Rooms are spacious, and shows run nearly every night.

Tropicana Atlantic City

A huge, all-in-one resort with The Quarter — a lively indoor shopping and dining complex. Ideal for those who want everything in one place: food, drinks, shops, casino, cinema, and beach access.

Cape May

The Virginia Hotel

A quiet, upscale stay in a restored 19th-century property. Expect attentive service, easy access to the beach, and a peaceful atmosphere just a short walk from the main shops.

La Mer Beachfront Resort

Clean, bright rooms with balconies that overlook the beach. A great mid-range option for travellers who want modern comfort and direct sand access.

Congress Hall

America’s oldest seaside resort, now fully restored with modern touches. Vibrant in summer, calm in the off-season, and central to everything Cape May has to offer.

The Southern Mansion

A grand, historic inn tucked away from the bustle but close enough to walk everywhere. Known for its unique rooms and relaxed garden setting.

Jersey Shore

The Asbury Hotel – Asbury Park

Creative, stylish, and walkable to everything. The rooftop bar, live events, and casual design make this a favourite for younger travellers and city day-trippers.

White Sands Oceanfront Resort – Point Pleasant Beach

One of the few resorts with a private beach, outdoor pool, and onsite spa. A full-service stay that keeps you close to boardwalk attractions.

Boardwalk Hotel Charlee – Seaside Heights

A straightforward, centrally located motel just steps from the rides, games, and food stands. Great for casual, fun-first beach weekends.

Berkeley Oceanfront Hotel – Asbury Park

A historic property with huge rooms, ocean views, and easy access to the convention centre and downtown area.

Hoboken

W Hoboken

The closest you can get to Manhattan without actually being in it. Sleek interiors, riverfront views, and walkable access to Hoboken’s cafes, bars, and waterfront park.

Sheraton Lincoln Harbor – Weehawken

Just outside Hoboken with quick ferry and transit access. Often more affordable than downtown Hoboken stays while keeping you close to the same attractions.

Short-Term Rentals

Hoboken has a wide selection of well-rated apartments and condos — ideal for longer stays or those who want their own kitchen and more privacy.

Princeton

Nassau Inn

Located right in Palmer Square, this inn is within walking distance of the university, art museum, and all of downtown’s restaurants and boutiques.

The Peacock Inn

A boutique, 16-room inn with stylish rooms and an on-site restaurant. Great for couples and travellers who prefer quiet refinement over larger hotels.

Hyatt Place Princeton

Located just outside the town centre with bigger rooms, reliable service, and free parking. Great for families or business travellers.

Asbury Park

The Asbury Hotel

Modern and social, with common areas designed for mingling. Rooftop movies, a pool deck, and music events make this one of the most active hotels in town.

Empress Hotel

Right across from the beach and nightlife spots. Known for being LGBTQ+ friendly, with a strong community vibe and great access to everything.

Hotel Tides

A smaller, boutique option with a more intimate setting, an on-site bar, and a rotating art gallery.

Montclair

The George

A design-led boutique hotel with individually styled rooms, built into a 1900s-era property. Ideal for creative travellers or weekenders who want more than just a bed.

The MC Hotel

A polished, modern hotel located just off Bloomfield Ave. Easy walking distance to restaurants, galleries, and coffee shops.

Local Rentals

Montclair’s homes and apartments are often available for short stays. Many are within walking distance of the town’s main districts.

The Pine Barrens

Batsto Guesthouse

Rustic and quiet, this guesthouse is minutes from Batsto Village and deep within Wharton State Forest — ideal for hikers and disconnecting fully.

Pilgrim Lake Campground

Cabins, tents, and full camp facilities for travellers who want an outdoors-first experience. Book early in peak months.

Private Rentals in Hammonton & Chatsworth

Secluded cabins and small homes available via rental platforms. Some include fire pits, decks, and easy access to trails or kayaking routes.

Delaware Water Gap

Shawnee Inn & Golf Resort

Just over the NJ/PA line, but close enough to count. Great for travellers looking to mix hiking and comfort — with river views and full resort facilities.

Water Gap Country Inn

A quiet inn near trailheads with simple, clean rooms and a low-key atmosphere. Ideal for fall travellers or long-weekend hikers.

Cabin Rentals

There are plenty of private rentals near the Water Gap — ranging from rustic lodges to modern retreats with forest views.

New Brunswick

The Heldrich Hotel

In the centre of downtown, across from the State Theatre and surrounded by restaurants and bars. Great for show weekends or university visits.

Hyatt Regency New Brunswick

Spacious rooms and modern design just off the main strip. Good for conferences, short stays, and travellers who want predictability with comfort.

Rutgers University Inn

Budget-friendly, quiet, and often overlooked. Ideal during breaks or for those visiting the university on a tight budget.
