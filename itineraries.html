New Jersey Itineraries

Trip Plans That Actually Work

New Jersey is easy to get around, but that doesn’t mean every trip is obvious. With so many beach towns, casinos, forests, small cities, and local gems, it’s hard to know how to fit it all into one route. That’s where we come in.

The itineraries below are designed to help you get the most out of your time — whether you’re here for a weekend or a full week. No wasted days, no overcomplication, just solid plans built around real places worth visiting.

Weekend in Atlantic City (2 Days)

Perfect for: Couples, groups, solo travellers

Focus: Casino hotels, entertainment, boardwalk, food

Day 1 – Arrival + Evening Out

  • Check into a casino hotel: Ocean Casino Resort for the views, Borgata for upscale, Hard Rock for live music.

  • Walk the boardwalk before sunset — grab a drink at LandShark Bar or Steel Pier Pub.

  • Dinner inside your resort (think: steakhouses, seafood spots, or sushi).

  • Hit the casino floor or catch a show — AC usually has live music, comedy, or DJs most nights.

Day 2 – Boardwalk + Relaxation

  • Early morning stroll on the boardwalk (sunrise if you’re up for it).

  • Breakfast at Gilchrist (locals love it) or grab a quick bite inside your hotel.

  • Optional spa visit or time on the beach (May–September)

  • Shop at Tanger Outlets before hitting the road home.

This trip works best Friday–Sunday but also fits midweek if you want better hotel rates.

3-Day Coastal Road Trip

Route: Atlantic City → Cape May → Asbury Park

Perfect for: Friends, couples, solo travellers

Focus: Beach towns, boardwalks, music, food, scenery

Day 1 – Atlantic City

  • Check in, explore the boardwalk, and enjoy a night at the casino.

  • Dine at Amada in Ocean or hit The Yard for a more casual feel.

Day 2 – Cape May

  • Drive 1 hour south on the Garden State Parkway

  • Visit the Cape May Lighthouse, walk through the historic district, and stop by a local winery.

  • Stay at a boutique inn or coastal motel.

  • Dinner near the marina or opt for a BYOB seafood restaurant downtown.

Day 3 – Asbury Park

  • Take the scenic route north (about 2 hours)

  • Explore the boardwalk murals, stop into indie shops, and check out the Silverball Museum Arcade.

  • If you stay overnight, there’s plenty of live music and late-night food options.

This trip delivers a perfect mix of laid-back beach stops and social energy.

1-Week New Jersey Highlights

Perfect for: Out-of-state visitors, road trippers, or anyone looking to explore it all

Focus: Coastal towns, small cities, outdoors, local culture

Day 1–2 – Atlantic City

  • Stay in a casino hotel

  • Relax on the beach, shop the outlets, and experience the nightlife

  • Add a spa session or walk the full length of the boardwalk

Day 3 – Cape May

  • Drive down to Cape May

  • Walk the historic district and hit Sunset Beach for views

  • Stay in a restored B&B or modern beachfront inn

Day 4 – Princeton

  • Drive inland (about 2.5 hrs)

  • Explore Princeton University, paddle on Carnegie Lake, visit the art museum

  • Stay in a historic inn or modern boutique hotel downtown

Day 5 – Montclair

  • Stop for lunch and galleries, coffee shops, record stores, and a walk around Edgemont Park

  • Spend a relaxed afternoon before heading north

Day 6–7 – Delaware Water Gap or Pine Barrens

  • Go full nature mode: hiking, river kayaking, waterfalls, or forest trails

  • Stay in a cabin, lodge, or simple motel near the trails

  • Wrap the trip with a slow morning and head back downstate

Couple’s Getaway (3–4 Days)

Perfect for: Romance, anniversaries, low-stress travel

Focus: Slow pace, walkable towns, local food

  • Start in Cape May: historic inns, carriage rides, waterfront restaurants

  • Day trip to Princeton for museums, coffee shops, and green spaces

  • End with a night in Atlantic City: book a couples spa, enjoy dinner and a show, and stay in a high-floor ocean-view suite

Best done in spring or autumn for fewer crowds and cooler weather.

Family Weekend Itinerary

Perfect for: Kids, teens, multigenerational groups

Focus: Beach towns, boardwalks, casual eats

Day 1 – Point Pleasant Beach

  • Check into a motel or family-friendly Airbnb

  • Hit the beach, the boardwalk, and Jenkinson’s Aquarium

  • Dinner at a pizza or burger spot — no shortage nearby

Day 2 – Seaside Heights

  • Mini golf in the morning

  • Arcade games, boardwalk snacks, and beach time in the afternoon

  • Arcade games, boardwalk snacks, and beach time in the afternoon

Most of the shore towns have family attractions — this one just keeps it simple and fun.

Autumn Road Trip (3 Days)

Perfect for: Nature lovers, photographers, couples

Focus: Foliage, trails, scenic drives

Day 1 – Princeton

  • Start with a morning walk around the university and lunch at a local café

  • Drive scenic backroads to enjoy early color changes

Day 2 – Delaware Water Gap

  • Hike to Mount Tammany, visit waterfalls, and take in views from the river valley

  • Stay in a rustic lodge or motel surrounded by forest

Day 3 – Montclair or Clinton

  • Head back south via Montclair for culture and food, or stop in Clinton to walk through the historic Red Mill area

  • Slow afternoon and relaxed drive home
