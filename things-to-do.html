Things to Do in New Jersey

New Jersey packs an incredible range of activities into a small space. With beaches, forests, casinos, boardwalks, historic towns, and urban escapes all within a few hours of each other, you’ll never be short on options. Whether you're looking for a quick weekend fix or planning a longer route through the state, there's plenty to see and do.

This guide breaks down the best experiences across the Garden State — from outdoor adventures to food, culture, and nightlife.

Casino Resorts and Entertainment

Atlantic City is the heart of New Jersey's casino scene. The beachfront strip is lined with full-scale casino hotels offering gaming, live shows, rooftop pools, restaurants, and spas. It's ideal for couples, groups, and anyone looking for a high-energy base on the coast.

Top resorts like Borgata, Ocean Casino, Hard Rock, Tropicana, and Caesars combine accommodation, nightlife, dining, and attractions under one roof. Even if you don’t plan to play, these properties offer everything you need for a short break.

Boardwalks and Shore Towns

New Jersey’s coastline stretches over 140 miles, with beach towns offering boardwalks, arcades, food stalls, and summer events. Spend the day riding a Ferris wheel in Seaside Heights, explore Asbury Park’s murals and music scene, or unwind with a beachside drink in Point Pleasant.

Each town brings a different atmosphere, but the classic boardwalk experience ties them all together — with games, coastal views, and laid-back energy throughout.

Nature and Outdoor Escapes

For fresh air and open space, head inland. The Delaware Water Gap offers some of the best hiking in the state, with scenic trails and mountain views. The Pine Barrens is a vast forested region full of quiet lakes, river routes, and folklore.

New Jersey is also home to state parks, mountain ridges, botanical gardens, and easy nature walks that work for all experience levels. Whether you're in hiking boots or flip-flops, there’s outdoor space for you here.

Food and Markets

New Jersey’s food scene blends old-school favourites with modern flavours. From classic diners and Italian-American comfort food to boardwalk seafood, international eats, and seasonal markets — the variety is huge.

Highlights include:

Italian delis and bakeries in Hoboken

Fresh seafood along the coast

High-end dining inside casino resorts

Brunch favourites in Montclair and New Brunswick

Seasonal farmers markets in towns across the state

Small Towns and Weekend Stops

New Jersey’s smaller towns are full of charm and character. Cape May offers coastal calm and historic homes. Princeton combines university history with great cafes and public parks. Montclair is known for its indie shops, galleries, and weekend events.

These are the kind of places that make perfect day trips or easy two-night breaks — simple to get to, easy to explore, and full of reasons to stay a little longer.

Arts, Music, and Nightlife

Live entertainment is a key part of the travel experience here. Atlantic City’s casinos bring big-name acts throughout the year, while Asbury Park is known for local venues and outdoor music events. Hoboken offers live shows and dive bars, and New Brunswick has a full theatre and concert calendar thanks to Rutgers.

From stage shows to basement gigs, you can build a night out around almost any destination on your itinerary.

History and Landmarks

History runs deep in New Jersey. Visit battlegrounds from the Revolutionary War, tour preserved homes in Cape May, or walk through museums covering everything from maritime history to aviation and pop culture.

In Princeton, you can walk the same campus paths as presidents and scholars. Elsewhere, lighthouses, forts, and monuments offer plenty of low-cost, high-value places to explore.

Seasonal Highlights

The calendar brings new reasons to visit throughout the year:

Spring: cherry blossoms, market season, garden walks

Summer: beach days, concerts, fireworks, boardwalk fun

Autumn: foliage drives, food festivals, outdoor events

Winter: indoor escapes, resort deals, holiday displays

Each season has its own rhythm — and planning around the right time can turn a good trip into a great one.
